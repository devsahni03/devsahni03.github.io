Retail Lending Risk Intelligence Platform

A portfolio-wide risk and recovery monitoring system built for retail lending leadership — covering 60,000+ loan accounts across product, region, and credit segment.

Power BI SQL DAX Power Query Excel

Executive Summary

Retail lending portfolios generate risk signals across dozens of disconnected reports — origination, collections, credit bureau data — none of which leadership could view together. This platform consolidates 60,000+ loan accounts into a single risk intelligence view, giving portfolio, risk, and collections leaders one place to answer the questions that matter: where is risk concentrating, is collections keeping pace, and which segments need a policy response. It replaces retrospective, siloed reporting with a live view of portfolio health.

Business Problem

Portfolio health was being assessed through static, product-specific reports that surfaced weeks after risk had already materialized. Regional and product-level NPA trends were invisible until quarter-end reviews, by which point underwriting or collections decisions were reactive rather than preventive. Without a segmented, real-time view of exposure and default patterns, the organization risked concentrating capital in higher-risk products and regions without knowing it — and reacting to bad debt instead of anticipating it.

Business Objectives

  • Improve visibility into overall portfolio health, in one consolidated view
  • Monitor credit risk consistently across loan products and regions
  • Track collection efficiency against outstanding exposure
  • Identify which lending segments carry disproportionate risk
  • Support underwriting and collections decisions with a single source of truth
  • Reduce the manual effort spent assembling portfolio reports

My Role

I owned this build end-to-end as the sole Business Analyst: defining what "portfolio health" needed to mean for this audience, designing the KPI set from scratch, cleaning and modeling the underlying loan data, building the reporting layer, and translating the resulting patterns into a set of business recommendations. The scope covered requirement definition, data preparation, KPI design, dashboard development, and stakeholder-ready reporting — not just the visual build.

Key Insights

  • Portfolio value exceeded ₹39B across 60,000+ loan accounts, with Home Loans alone accounting for roughly half of total portfolio value despite a smaller share of account count
  • Collection efficiency held above 92%, indicating a fundamentally healthy book despite a mixed-risk product portfolio
  • Consumer Durable and Business Loans carried the highest default risk — NPA rates roughly 3x that of Home Loans
  • Credit score was the single strongest predictor of default: sub-600 score accounts defaulted at a rate over 20x higher than 800+ accounts
  • East and Central regions carried disproportionately higher NPA exposure relative to their share of disbursed volume

Business Recommendations

  • Strengthen underwriting criteria for Consumer Durable and Business Loan products specifically, rather than applying a blanket policy change
  • Increase monitoring frequency for borrowers below a 650 credit score, where risk accelerates sharply
  • Prioritize collections resourcing toward East and Central regions given their disproportionate NPA share
  • Introduce an early-warning trigger on vintage cohorts showing rising 30-60 DPD rates, before they reach NPA status
  • Review approval thresholds specifically for the lowest two credit-score bands rather than portfolio-wide

Business Impact

  • Centralized portfolio monitoring into a single, consistent view across product and region
  • Improved executive visibility into risk concentration that was previously fragmented across reports
  • Enabled faster, segment-specific decisions instead of reactive, portfolio-wide policy changes
  • Reduced the manual effort required to assemble a portfolio health view for leadership review
  • Supported risk-based lending and collections prioritization with a repeatable, auditable data model

Executive KPIs

Portfolio Value
Signals growth and market share — but only meaningful when read alongside risk-adjusted metrics, not on its own.
Outstanding Exposure
Quantifies live capital at risk at any given point in time, independent of how much was originally disbursed.
Collection Efficiency
The earliest signal of book health — deteriorates before NPA numbers do, giving leadership a head start.
NPA Rate
The definitive scorecard on underwriting quality, viewed by product and segment rather than as one blended number.
Average Credit Score
A leading indicator of future portfolio risk, not just a description of current performance.
Recoverable Amount
Quantifies the specific, actionable upside available through underwriting or collections policy change.
Write Off Amount
The realized cost of risk that underwriting and collections were ultimately unable to prevent.

Challenges

  • Reconciling data quality inconsistencies across loan, borrower, and collections records
  • Balancing a portfolio spanning six distinct loan products, each with different risk drivers
  • Standardizing KPI definitions so "risk" and "recovery" meant the same thing across every report
  • Designing a single dashboard that served both executive summary needs and operational drill-down
  • Building DAX measures that stayed accurate under every possible filter combination, not just the default view

Learnings

This project sharpened how I think about a dataset before I touch a chart — starting from what decision a metric needs to support, not what the data happens to contain. It reinforced that risk analytics is a storytelling problem as much as a technical one: the same NPA number means something different to an underwriter than to an investor, and the reporting has to serve both. It also taught me to design KPIs for correctness under every filter state, not just the default view — a mistake in that logic is invisible until someone actually filters the report.

Tools

  • SQL — the analytical backbone: segmented risk and recovery patterns across 60,000+ records before any visual was built
  • DAX — translated that SQL logic into live, filter-aware measures inside the report itself
  • Power Query — handled data cleaning and shaping ahead of modeling, so the DAX layer stayed simple
  • Power BI — the reporting layer stakeholders actually interact with, built for both executive and operational use
  • Excel — used for quick validation checks against the SQL and DAX outputs before publishing

Final Outcome

This project reflects how I approach analysis generally: start from the business question, not the dataset, and design backward from the decision a stakeholder actually needs to make. The technical build — the SQL, the DAX, the dashboard itself — is the visible output, but the real work was translating a scattered, reactive reporting process into a single, decision-ready view of portfolio risk. That's the capability this project is meant to demonstrate: not that I can build a dashboard, but that I know what a dashboard needs to answer before I start building it.
